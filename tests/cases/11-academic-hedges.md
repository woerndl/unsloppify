# Case 11: Academic abstract where hedges are content
Context: academic
## Input
## Abstract

We investigated whether intermittent fasting affects markers of insulin sensitivity in a cohort of 84 adults over 12 weeks. Participants were randomized to either a time-restricted eating window or an unrestricted control. Our results suggest that the intervention group showed a modest improvement in fasting glucose, though the effect did not reach statistical significance (p = 0.08). These findings may indicate a small benefit, but the sample size limits the conclusions we can draw. Further research with larger cohorts is needed to determine whether the effect is robust. We note several limitations, including reliance on self-reported adherence and the relatively short study duration. The results should be interpreted with caution and do not support broad dietary recommendations at this stage.
## Must flag
- none
## Must not flag
- "Our results suggest that" (academic hedge, appropriate to a non-significant result)
- "These findings may indicate a small benefit" (hedge is content, the uncertainty is the finding)
- "Further research with larger cohorts is needed" (grounded in the stated sample-size limit)
- "whether the effect is robust" (domain register, standard statistical usage rather than a gravitas word)
- "should be interpreted with caution" (hedge is content, grounded in named limitations)
- "(p = 0.08)" (concrete statistic)
